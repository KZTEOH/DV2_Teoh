World Happiness Report

In a world constantly chasing success and wealth, the pursuit of happiness stands as the most coveted treasure. It's the elusive treasure we all seek, transcending cultural, geographic, and individual boundaries. But what exactly is happiness, and where do we find it?

In this visualisation, you can have an overview the state of global happiness, identify the contribution of happiness in different region in the world, and discover the relationship between different happiness attributes - economic production, social support, life expectancy, freedom, absence of corruption, and generosity.


Where is Happiness?

Everyone in the world looking for happiness, but where can we find it?

The World Happiness Report is a landmark survey in 2015 which ranks 159 countries by their happiness levels on the 0 to 10 scale, review the state of happiness in the world today and show how the new science of happiness explains personal and national variations in happiness.

The maps shows gives the happiness score of different countries around the world based on seven factors including Economy, Family, Life Expectancy, Freedom, Trust in Government, Generosity, Dystopia Residual. Sum of the value of these seven factors gives us the happiness score and the higher the happiness score, the lower the happiness rank. So, it is evident that the higher value of each of these seven factors means the level of happiness is higher.

Colors from this map reflects the level of happiness. We can see that happiness are not distributed equally for each country.

Ranking at top 1 is Switzerland, with a top score of 7.587 folllowed by Iceland and Denmark.

We can also identify some happiness leaders regions - North America, Western Europe, Australia and New Zealand from the map.

But, which region have higher score than the others? And why are they more happy?


Which Region are more Happy?

This stacked bar chart compares the average happiness score for different regions with differnt happiness attributes.

From the average score North America, Australia and New Zealand ranks the happiest region in the world, with a average score of 7.287 and 7. 7.285.

By comparing the region against the happiness attribute, Australia ranks the top for most of the attribute, such as Economy, Family, Health, Freedom, Trust, Generosity, whereas North Ameriaca only ranks top for the Dystopia Residual.

In term of regions that has the lowest happiness score, Sub-Saharan Africa and Southern Asia has an average score of 4.519 and 4.521.

While comparing the happiness score among the region, we may ask: What exactly affects the happiness score and the rank? What attribute makes us happy the most?



What makes us Happy?

This pie chart shows how much each happineess attribute affect the overall score of a region.

Dystopia Residual is found out to be the main contribution to the happiness score in all the region. Dystopia Residual is just the residual metric to reflect other key variables for each country.

In investigating the main attributes, Economy which represents money and Family which represents relationships plays the most important role in happiness.

In the modern and busy world today, is money really that important for happiness? Or the relationships with family and friends is the key to be happy?


Will Money buy Happiness?

The age-old question of whether money can buy happiness continues to captivate minds and spark debates. Does financial wealth lead to contentment, or is happiness found in relationships?

To explore this, a bubble plot which draw the relationship between happineess score and Economy which indicates how rich a country is, with the size of the bubbles varies by Family which represents social relationships.

As shown in the chart, the plot has a slightly positive trend, indicating that Economy or money does contribute to the overall score. Whilst wealth can influence happiness score to an extend, Family attribute (which represented by the size of the circle) plays a very important part in happineess too!

From the visualisation, we can see that countrys with lower Economic production can still achieve the same or high level of happiness with the influence of Family. For example, Mozambique is a low-eonomic (Economy: 0.083, Family: 1.026) country but still manage to be a lot more happy than Syria (Economy: 0.663, Family: 0.4756) due to the influence of Family. In the comparision for high happiness score country, the richest country, Qatar (Economy: 1.690, Family: 1.079) still acheive the same level of happiness as Brazil which is not that (Economy: 0.981, Family: 1.233). This show that money can't buy happineess!

While money can certainly enhance our quality of life by providing comfort and opportunities, it is often the quality of our relationships that truly enriches our existence. These bonds bring emotional support, love, and a sense of belonging that money alone cannot buy. Finding the right balance between financial stability and nurturing meaningful connections is the key to a fulfilling and happy life.
