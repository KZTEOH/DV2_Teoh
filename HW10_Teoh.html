World Happiness Report in 2015

The World Happiness Report is a landmark survey of the state of global happiness. The World Happiness 2015, which ranks 159 countries by their happiness levels, was released at the United Nations at an event celebrating International Day of Happiness. The report continues to gain global recognition as governments, organizations and civil society increasingly use happiness indicators to inform their policy-making decisions. Leading experts across fields: economics, psychology, survey analysis, national statistics, health, public policy and more, describe how measurements of well-being can be used effectively to assess the progress of nations. The reports review the state of happiness in the world today and show how the new science of happiness explains personal and national variations in happiness.

Does Money Bring Happiness?

Can we find correlation between wealth and happiness? To investigate that, we can use the World Happiness dataset, and perform a analysis with the countrys GDP per capita, which is a greater indicator of how rich a country is.

Already at first glance, the graph seems to have an upwards trend. Suggesting a positive correlation between GDP per capita and happiness. Yes, MORE MONEY MORE HAPPY!
